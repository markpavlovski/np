Groton, Massachusetts

Groton, Massachuestts is a scenic and historic town located in North Central Massachusetts. You may wish to combine your trip to Nashoba Paddler with other area activities. When you visit our town, we hope you take the opportunity to learn some it's history and enjoy some of it's scenary. Groton was an early American settlement and the history of the cleanup of the Nashua River is featured in books and films.

Activities

The Nashua River Rail Trail

The Nashua River Rail Trail starts in Ayer, Massachusetts and passes through Groton, Pepperell, and Dunstable. It terminates at the New Hampshire border. It is a popular trail for biking, roller blading and certain sections allow for horseback riding. Out of town visitors may want to combine a half day paddler on the Nashua River with a half day of biking along the Nashua River! For information on where to park to use the trail see .... or speak to one of our employees when you arrive.

Groton Conservation Properties

The town of Groton and various conservation groups have set aside quite a bit of land. There is an active Trails Committee which maintains various trails throughout the town.

Area Restaurants

Groton and the surrounding towns have plenty of options for places to eat before or after your paddle. We've listed a few of your options but there are many others.

Clover Farm Market

Just down the road from our site is in the quaint center of West Groton is the Clover Farm Market. You can get a lunch to go for your paddle or sit across the street in the small park by the edge of the Squannacook River. They also serve breakfast sandwishes and coffee. (Closed on Sundays.)

Filho's Cantina

Located on Main Street in Groton at the intersection with 225, this popular restaurant has tables inside and outside. It tends to b e crowded in the evenings so you may want to phone in your order ahead. (Closed on Mondays.)

Johnson's Drivein

Johnsons is located on Main Street (Route 119) east of the center of town directly across from the Groton Country Club. It serves Ice Cream as well as breakfast and lunch. They serve sandwiches, hamburgers and have picnic table seating both inside and out.

The Main Street Cafe

Before coming to town, check their schedule to see if they have any live music! Located right in the center of Groton, they are a good stopoff to get a cup of coffee or a bite to eat on your way into town.

The Natural Market

Located across the street from the Main Street Cafe, this Health Food store serves sandwiches.

The Gibett Hill Grill

The Gibett Hill Grill is a bit more refined restaurant with a beautiful view of the surrounding hillside. Reservations would definitely be suggested as this is a very popular restaurant and a destination in itself.

Brunos Pizza

Located on Main Street near the center of town, Bruno's serves Italian style thin crust pizza. In addition to their indoor seating, there are few tables outside where you can sit directly on main street.

Blackbird Cafe

Located in Mill Street Plazza just west of Groton off 119 is an Internet Cafe that serves soups, salads for lunch.

Groton Wellness Bistro

Located on right next to the Blackbird Cafe specializes in healthly options for lunch.

Bailey's Grille

Located just west of Groton on Route 119 in Townsend, Bailey's has a bar area in addtion to their dining area. They also have an outdoor dining area for those nice summer evenings.

These are just a few suggestions. As you head through Ayer, Shirley, Devens, Littleton and Pepperell you will find other restaurants. We keep a selection of menus at the rental stand that you can look through.
